### 3. Saga Pattern — could add Orchestration vs Choreography
The page mentions Sagas briefly but doesn't break down the two implementation styles. Modern practice distinguishes:
- **Choreography** (event-based, decentralized, each service publishes/consumes events) — harder to trace, looser coupling
- **Orchestration** (central coordinator/saga manager tells each service what to do) — easier to monitor, more coupling
Given you use **Temporal** in your stack, the orchestration approach would be a natural fit to mention.
Refs: https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/architecture/patterns/saga, https://blog.bytebytego.com/p/saga-pattern-demystified-orchestration

### 4. Connection Pooling — could mention PgBouncer vs Odyssey
The page describes connection pooling at a high level (correctly) but doesn't mention specific poolers. Key developments:
- **PgBouncer 1.25.0** (Nov 2025) added LDAP auth support and direct TLS connections
- **Odyssey** (modern pooler by Yandex, multi-threaded) is a notable alternative
- Pool modes (session vs transaction pooling) nuance is worth covering
Refs: https://www.pgbouncer.org/, https://www.mafiree.com/blog/postgresql-connection-pooling-pgbouncer-vs-odyssey
