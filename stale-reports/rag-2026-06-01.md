## Summary

This page is **partially stale**. The overall architecture (ingestion → chunking → embedding → retrieval → reranking → generation) is solid and many design decisions remain current. However, several components need updates: the embedding model (nomic-embed-text v1 has been superseded by v1.5 and v2-moe), the reranker (cross-encoder/ms-marco-MiniLM-L6-v2 is outdated compared to BGE Reranker v2-m3 or Jina Reranker v2), the generator models (Gemma 3 is superseded by Gemma 4 as of April 2026; Phi-4 mini remains current), and the surprisingly small context budget (2800 tokens for a 4K window — most modern models support 128K+). The page also lacks coverage of major 2025–2026 RAG trends: GraphRAG, Agentic RAG, and semantic/agentic chunking strategies.

## What's Still Accurate

- **Overall RAG pipeline architecture** — the ingest → chunk → embed → retrieve → rerank → generate flow remains the standard design pattern.
- **Hybrid search (dense + sparse)** — this is still best practice; RRF fusion with k=60 is a well-proven method.
- **Qdrant as the vector store** — Qdrant v1.18.1 (May 2026) is actively maintained and the choice of Qdrant is sound. The payload filtering, full-text indexing, and native RRF support described are all current features.
- **Semantic cache** — the concept of caching by query embedding similarity is still valid and widely adopted.
- **HyDE and its variants** — Standard, Adaptive, and Multi-HyDE remain relevant research directions (Adaptive HyDE ref arxiv 2507.16754 and Multi-HyDE ref arxiv 2509.16369 are from 2025).
- **"Lost in the Middle" chunk reordering** — Liu et al. 2023 findings on position bias are still widely cited.
- **Post-retrieval LLM chunk filtering** — the technique described (arxiv 2410.19572) is still valid.
- **Ollama as local LLM provider** — Ollama is still the dominant local inference tool (v0.30.0-rc31 as of May 2026).
- **Docling for document processing** — Docling is very actively maintained (v2.96+ as of June 2026).
- **SPLADE for sparse retrieval** — SPLADE continues to be the standard sparse neural retrieval model.

## Potential Updates Needed

1. **Embedding model: nomic-embed-text v1 → v1.5 or v2-moe**
   - The page uses `nomic-embed-text` (v1) with 768d. Nomic released **v1.5** (Feb 2024, widely adopted with 17.7M downloads) and **nomic-embed-text-v2-moe** (Feb 2025, MoE architecture with 1.14M downloads). The v2-moe model offers improved performance, multilingual support, and is available in GGUF format for Ollama. Consider updating the config to use `nomic-embed-text-v1.5` or `v2-moe`.
   - Source: https://huggingface.co/nomic-ai/nomic-embed-text-v1.5, https://huggingface.co/nomic-ai/nomic-embed-text-v2-moe

2. **Reranker model is outdated**
   - `cross-encoder/ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2` (2022) is the most downloaded cross-encoder (69M) but newer models offer better RAG performance: **BAAI/bge-reranker-v2-m3** (13.8M downloads, multilingual, specifically designed for retrieval), **jinaai/jina-reranker-v2-base-multilingual** (1.6M downloads, 2024), and **BAAI/bge-reranker-large** (1.5M downloads). The BGE reranker v2-m3 is widely considered the current state-of-the-art for open-source reranking in RAG pipelines.
   - Source: https://huggingface.co/BAAI/bge-reranker-v2-m3, https://huggingface.co/jinaai/jina-reranker-v2-base-multilingual

3. **Generator models are partially outdated**
   - **Gemma 3** (used as `gemma3:1b` for HyDE and chunk filter) was superseded by **Gemma 4** on April 2, 2026 (Apache 2.0, available in E2B, E4B, 26B MoE, and 31B Dense variants with multimodal vision+audio support). The 1B variant of Gemma 3 may no longer be the best small model.
   - **Phi-4 mini** (used as `phi4-mini:latest`) remains current and well-regarded for complex reasoning.
   - Consider adding support for newer small models like **Llama 4 Maverick** (April 2025, 17B-128E MoE) or **Muse Spark** (Meta, April 2026).
   - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gemma_(language_model)

4. **Context window budget is far too small**
   - The page defaults to 2800 tokens (~70% of 4K context). In 2025–2026, virtually every model supports **128K+ context windows** (Gemma 3/4: 128K, Phi-4: 128K, Llama 4: 1M). The "Lost in the Middle" problem still applies, but the token budget should be increased significantly (e.g., 16K–32K default) and the context length should be configurable per model rather than hardcoded to 4K.

5. **Missing: GraphRAG**
   - Microsoft Research's **GraphRAG** (introduced 2024, refined through 2025–2026) is a major development that extends RAG with knowledge graphs. It allows LLMs to synthesize knowledge graph structures from documents and perform global search over summarized communities. Many practitioners now consider GraphRAG complementary to traditional RAG for tasks requiring global understanding.
   - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prompt_engineering (mentions GraphRAG by Microsoft Research), https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_graph

6. **Missing: Agentic RAG**
   - **Agentic RAG** (massive trend in 2025–2026) extends RAG with agentic loops: multi-step retrieval, tool use, self-reflection, and answer verification. Companies like Contextual AI and Progress (Nuclia acquisition) are building in this space. The page's current design is a single-pass RAG; adding an agent loop section would make it more modern.
   - Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douwe_Kiela (Contextual AI CEO on Agentic RAG), Wikipedia search results for "Progress snaps up Nuclia for agentic RAG tech"

7. **Missing: Advanced chunking strategies**
   - The page covers Recursive and Sentence Window chunking. Since 2025, **semantic chunking** (using embedding similarity to detect topic boundaries), **agentic chunking** (LLM-driven content-aware splitting), and **late chunking** (chunking after encoding, proposed by Jina AI) have become best practices.

8. **Missing: RAG evaluation and benchmarking**
   - The doc mentions evaluation in the first line but has no section on evaluation frameworks. Tools like **RAGAS**, **TruLens**, **ARES**, and the **MTEB RAG benchmark** are now standard for measuring RAG quality (retrieval precision, answer faithfulness, context relevance).

9. **Docling version not pinned**
   - Docling is under extremely active development (v2.96.1 as of June 1, 2026). The page doesn't pin a version, which could lead to breakage. Recommend pinning to a specific major version.
