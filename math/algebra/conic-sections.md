# Conic Sections

### Conic Sections

<table><thead><tr><th width="205.60003662109375">Shape</th><th>Equation (Standard Form)</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Circle</td><td><span class="math">(x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2</span></td></tr><tr><td>Ellipse</td><td><span class="math">\frac{(x-h)^2}{a^2} + \frac{(y-k)^2}{b^2} = 1</span></td></tr><tr><td>Hyperbola</td><td><span class="math">\frac{(x-h)^2}{a^2} - \frac{(y-k)^2}{b^2} = 1</span></td></tr></tbody></table>

$$(x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2$$

every point $$(x, y)$$ that lies on the edge of a specific circle

$$(h, k)$$: These are the coordinates of the Center of the circle.

* $$h$$ is the x-coordinate (left/right).
* $$k$$ is the y-coordinate (up/down).

$$r$$: This is the Radius (the distance from the center to the edge).

* Note: In the equation, the radius is squared ($$r^2$$).

$$(x, y)$$: These represent any point on the boundary of the circle.

If a circle is centered at $$(0, 0)$$ with a radius of $$5$$:

* $$(x - 0)^2 + (y - 0)^2 = 5^2$$
* $$x^2 + y^2 = 25$$
